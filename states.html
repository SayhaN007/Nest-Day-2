Kerala

Known as "God's Own Country," Kerala offers serene backwaters, lush greenery, pristine beaches, hill stations like Munnar, and rich cultural experiences such as Kathakali performances and Ayurvedic treatments. Famous destinations include Alleppey, Wayanad, Thekkady, and Kochi.

Goa

Renowned for its vibrant nightlife, golden beaches, and Portuguese heritage, Goa is a hotspot for both leisure and adventure. Attractions include popular beaches like Baga and Calangute, historic churches like the Basilica of Bom Jesus, and spice plantations.

Rajastan

Known as the "Land of Kings," Rajasthan showcases grand palaces, majestic forts, and desert landscapes. Key destinations include Jaipur, Udaipur, Jodhpur, Jaisalmer, and Ranthambore for its tiger reserve. It’s famous for its royal heritage, camel safaris, and folk music.

Himachal Pradesh

A paradise for nature and adventure lovers, Himachal Pradesh features scenic hill stations like Shimla, Manali, and Dharamshala, as well as trekking trails and river rafting in the Himalayas. The state also offers serene monasteries and vibrant festivals.

Uttar Pradesh

Uttar Pradesh is a cultural and spiritual hub, home to the iconic Taj Mahal in Agra, the sacred ghats of Varanasi, and the vibrant city of Lucknow known for its Nawabi heritage. It’s also significant for its association with Hindu and Buddhist pilgrimages.

Gujarat

Known for its cultural diversity and heritage, Gujarat features the Gir National Park (home to Asiatic lions), the white salt desert of the Rann of Kutch, and historic sites like the Somnath Temple and the Sun Temple at Modhera. The Sabarmati Ashram and vibrant festivals like Navratri also draw tourists.

Punjab

Punjab is celebrated for its rich history, vibrant culture, and culinary delights. The Golden Temple in Amritsar is a major attraction, along with the Wagah Border ceremony, historic sites in Ludhiana and Patiala, and the lush countryside dotted with mustard fields.
